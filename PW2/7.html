Briards

A HEART WRAPPED IN FUR

The Briad, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally
used as a herder and guardian of sheep

Breed History

The briad, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World War almost to the point of extiniction. Currently the population of briards is slowly recovering. Charlemange, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briads.
